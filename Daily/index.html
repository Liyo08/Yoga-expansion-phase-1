First Move

Pranamasana (Prayer Pose)

Click above ⬆️

Stand straight with feet together. Bring your palms together in a prayer position at the chest. Keep your shoulders relaxed and breathe normally. This pose helps calm the mind and prepares the body for the sequence.

Second Move

Hastauttanasana (Raised Arms Pose)

Click above ⬆️

Inhale and raise both arms overhead. Stretch backward slightly, arching your spine. Keep the arms close to the ears, and engage your core. This stretches the abdominal muscles and improves digestion.

Third Move

Padahastasana (Standing Forward Bend)

Click above ⬆️

Exhale and bend forward from the hips. Keep your spine straight as you lower your hands to touch the floor (or reach as far as possible). Try to keep your knees straight. This stretches the hamstrings, calves, and spine.

Fourth Move

Ashwa Sanchalanasana (Equestrian Pose)

Click above ⬆️

Inhale and step your right leg back. Place your right knee on the floor and keep your left knee bent at a 90-degree angle. Look up slightly and open the chest. This strengthens the legs and improves hip flexibility.

Fifth Move

Dandasana (Plank Pose)

Click above ⬆️

Exhale and step your left leg back to join the right. Keep the body in a straight line from head to heels. Engage your core and hold the position. This strengthens the arms, shoulders, and core.

Sixth Move

Ashtanga Namaskara (Eight-Limbed Pose)

Click above ⬆️

Exhale and lower the body, touching the chin, chest, hands, knees, and toes on the floor while keeping the hips slightly raised. Keep elbows close to the body. This increases flexibility and prepares the body for deeper backbends.

Seventh Move

Bhujangasana (Cobra Pose)

Click above ⬆️

Inhale and press your hands into the ground, lifting the chest. Keep elbows slightly bent and shoulders away from the ears. Look up and stretch the back. This strengthens the spine and opens the chest.

Eight Move

Adho Mukha Svanasana (Downward-Facing Dog)

Click above ⬆️

Exhale and lift your hips up, straightening your legs. Keep your hands and feet firmly on the ground. Try to push the heels toward the floor. This strengthens the arms and stretches the back and hamstrings.

Ninth Move

Ashwa Sanchalanasana (Equestrian Pose)-> Left Leg

Click above ⬆️

Inhale and step the right foot forward (same as Step 4, but with the opposite leg). Keep the left knee on the ground and look up. This improves flexibility and balance.

Tenth Move

Padahastasana (Standing Forward Bend)

Click above ⬆️

Exhale and bring the left foot forward to join the right. Keep your hands near your feet and head close to the knees. This stretches the hamstrings and relaxes the spine.

Eleventh Move

Hastauttanasana (Raised Arms Pose)

Click above ⬆️

Inhale and rise up, raising your arms overhead. Stretch backward slightly, keeping the arms close to the ears. Engage your core and lengthen the spine.

Twelth Move

Pranamasana (Prayer Pose)

Click above ⬆️

Exhale and bring your palms together in a prayer position at your chest. Stand tall and breathe deeply. This helps relax the body and brings awareness to the breath.
